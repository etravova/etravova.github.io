Welcome

I am an applied economist studying crime, religion, and political economy across both contemporary and historical settings. My research focuses on how institutions, incentives, and beliefs shape human behavior and long-run socio-economic outcomes.

Previously, I was a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Center for Economic Behavior and Inequality (CEBI), University of Copenhagen. I received my PhD in Economics and Econometrics from CERGE-EI (2022).
